Beef Wellington

This beef Wellington recipe makes an impressive beef tenderloin wrapped in puff pastry and includes how to make a rich red wine sauce to serve with it. It's easier than you think to make and perfect for holiday entertaining! The beef tenderloin is best served medium-rare.

This classic Beef Wellington recipe is perfect for any special occasion, from holidays to anniversaries.

What Is Beef Wellington?

Beef Wellington is a luxurious main dish made with beef tenderloin coated with paté and mushrooms, wrapped in puff pastry, and baked until the pastry is a beautiful golden brown. The classic English dish is named after Arthur Wellesley, 1st Duke of Wellington.

Ingredients

  • 2 ½ pounds beef tenderloin
  • 4 tablespoons butter, softened, divided
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • ½ cup sliced fresh mushrooms
  • 2 ounces liver paté
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 (17.5 ounce) package frozen puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 large egg yolk, beaten
  • 1 (10.5 ounce) can beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons red wine

Steps

  1. Preheat Oven: Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Prepare Beef: Place the beef tenderloin in a baking dish. Spread 2 tablespoons of softened butter over the beef.
  3. Bake Beef: Bake in the preheated oven until browned, about 10 to 15 minutes. Remove the beef from the pan, reserve the pan juices, and let the beef cool completely.
  4. Increase Oven Temperature: Increase the oven temperature to 450°F (230°C).
  5. Sauté Vegetables: Melt 2 tablespoons of butter in a skillet over medium heat. Sauté the onion and mushrooms in the butter for 5 minutes, then remove from heat and let them cool.
  6. Prepare Paté Mixture: Mix paté and the remaining 2 tablespoons of softened butter in a bowl. Season with salt and pepper. Spread the paté mixture over the beef and top it with the onion and mushroom mixture.
  7. Roll Out Puff Pastry: Roll out the puff pastry dough to about 1/4-inch thickness and place the beef in the centre.
  8. Wrap Beef: Fold up and seal all the edges of the pastry, ensuring the seams are not too thick. Place the wrapped beef in a 9x13-inch baking dish. Cut a few slits in the top of the dough and brush with egg yolk.
  9. Bake Wellington: Bake in the preheated oven for 10 minutes. Reduce the heat to 425°F (220°C) and continue baking until the pastry is golden brown, about 10 to 15 minutes. Use an instant-read thermometer to check the centre; it should read 122°F to 130°F (50°C to 54°C) for medium rare. Let it rest.
  10. Prepare Sauce: Place the reserved pan juices in a small saucepan over high heat. Stir in beef broth and red wine, and boil until slightly reduced, about 5 minutes. Strain the sauce.
  11. Serve: Serve the Beef Wellington hot with the prepared sauce. Enjoy!
